Frequently Asked Questions

  • What is a pre-trial conference?
    A pre-trial conference is a meeting between the parties involved in a legal case, typically held before the trial, to discuss the case's status, potential settlement, and other matters related to the trial.
  • Why is a pre-trial conference important?
    Web Portal Usage: Pre-trial conferences help hasten the trial process by resolving disputes, setting trial dates, and encouraging settlements, ultimately saving time and resources for both the court.
  • How do I access the Pre-Trial Conference Web Portal?
    To access the web portal, visit our official website “URL DAALNA” and log in using your registered credentials.
  • What can I do on the web portal?
    Our portal allows users to schedule pre-trial conferences, view upcoming conference details, communicate with other parties involved, upload necessary documents, and track the progress of each case.
  • How do I schedule a pre-trial conference using the portal?
    Log in to the portal, navigate to the "Schedule" section, fill in the required details, select a suitable date and time, and submit the request. The system will notify all relevant parties and confirm the scheduling.
